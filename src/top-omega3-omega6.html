Introduction

The omega-3 fatty acids are long-chain polyunsaturated fatty acids including: alpha-linolenic acid (ALA), eicosapentaenoic acid (EPA), and docosahexaenoic acid (DHA). (Tomczyk 2018) The omega-6 fatty acids include linoleic acid (LA) and arachidonic acid (AA).

In the United States, the National Academy of Medicine has designated ALA and LA as essential nutrients. In the European Union, the European Food Safety Authority has designated ALA, LA, EPA and DHA as essential nutrients. The omega-3 fatty acids EPA and DHA have been shown to promote health. (Mousa 2012)

Paleolithic diets are estimated to have contained approximately equal amounts of omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids (i.e. an omega-3:omega-6 ratio of 1:1), whereas modern diets are much higher in omega-6 and much lower in omega-3 (a ratio of 1:10-20). (Simopoulos 2011)

Restoring an omega-3 to omega-6 ratio of 1:1 in one's diet requires data about the ratios in foods. Here we present a table of the top 1000 foods sorted by omega-3 to omega-6 ratio, as well as the top 100 foods in each of 24 food groups.
