Introduction

The brain-selective nutrients are those nutrients that are necessary for optimal brain development, namely: the minerals iodine, iron, zinc, copper and selenium; the vitamins A and D; and the fatty acid DHA. (Cunnane 2014)

Which foods are richest in the brain-selective nutrients? To answer this question objectively we derive a formula, the Brain-Selective Nutrient Index, and apply it to nutritional databases.

Derivation of the Brain-Selective Nutrient Index formula

To obtain a single measure of a food's content in multiple nutrients, we use a product. A sum would produce a measure of a food's content in the first nutrient or the second nutrient, or the third, and so on. A product produces a measure of a food's content in the first nutrient and the second nutrient, and the third, and so on. Thus the Brain-Selective Nutrient Index (BSNI) can be written as \[ BSNI = x_1 \times x_2 \times ... \times x_n \] where $x_i$ represent the values of each brain-specific nutrient in a portion of a food: $x_1=\text{iodine}$, $x_2=\text{iron}$, etc.

However, nutritional databases are not complete, not all nutrients have been measured in all foods. As we must use a value of zero for missing values, a single missing value would result in a product of zero, regardless of the values of every other nutrient. To solve this problem, we set the minimum of each term in the product to 1, by simply adding 1 to each term. Thus, \[ BSNI = (1 + x_1) \times (1 + x_2) \times ... \times (1 + x_n) \]

Finally, we must account for differences in units, otherwise nutrients required in milligram amounts would be more "weighty" in the formula than those in micrograms. So we normalize each nutrient value with a factor proportional to its unit of measure. For example if the nutrient $x_1$ is measured in milligrams it would be divided by $10^{-3}$, and if $x_2$ is measured in micrograms it would be divided by $10^{-6}$. So we obtain \[ BSNI = \left(1 + \frac{x_1}{u_1}\right) \times \left(1 + \frac{x_2}{u_2}\right) \times ... \times \left(1 + \frac{x_n}{u_n}\right) \]

Finally, we arrive at the formula \[ \begin{align} BSNI &= \left( 1 + \frac{I}{10^{-6}} \right) \times \left( 1 + \frac{Fe}{10^{-3}} \right) \times \left( 1 + \frac{Zn}{10^{-3}} \right) \times \left( 1 + \frac{Cu}{10^{-3}} \right) \\ &\times \left( 1 + \frac{Se}{10^{-6}} \right) \times \left( 1 + \frac{A}{10^{-6}} \right) \times \left( 1 + \frac{D}{10^{-6}} \right) \times \left( 1 + \frac{DHA}{10^{-3}} \right) \end{align} \] \[ \begin{align} I &: \text{iodine} \\ Fe &: \text{iron} \\ Zn &: \text{zinc} \\ Cu &: \text{copper} \\ Se &: \text{selenium} \\ A &: \text{vitamin A} \\ D &: \text{vitamin D} \\ DHA &: \text{docosahexaenoic acid} \end{align} \]
